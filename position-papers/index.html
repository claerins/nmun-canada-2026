Delegation overview Support the delegation

General Assembly First Committee (GA1)

Open PDF Go to committee

The First Committee focuses on disarmament and international security. It debates risks to peace and stability, including weapons issues and broader security challenges, and develops recommendations that are later negotiated in resolutions.

Delegates: Ekaterina Konovalova, Lanie Loedding.

For committee context, contacts, and delegate profiles, see the GA1 section on the delegation page.

General Assembly Second Committee (GA2)

Open PDF Go to committee

The Second Committee covers economic and financial questions. It addresses development-related policy priorities, international economic cooperation, and the financial and structural conditions that shape inclusive and sustainable growth.

Delegates: Mariana Avelar, Soehne Ivana Satya.

For committee context, contacts, and delegate profiles, see the GA2 section on the delegation page.

General Assembly Third Committee (GA3)

Open PDF Go to committee

The Third Committee deals with social, humanitarian, and cultural issues, including human rights. It discusses global policy challenges that affect individuals and communities, and develops recommendations that feed into General Assembly outcomes.

Delegates: Ulduz Bayramova, Clara Trebes.

For committee context, contacts, and delegate profiles, see the GA3 section on the delegation page.

Commission on Narcotic Drugs (CND)

Open PDF Go to committee

The Commission on Narcotic Drugs is the UN’s central policy-making body on drug-related matters within the UN system. It reviews global trends, sets policy direction, and considers measures to address the world drug situation through international cooperation.

Delegates: Velin Vaklinov Hristov, Anna-Marlen Thamm.

For committee context, contacts, and delegate profiles, see the CND section on the delegation page.

UN Economic and Social Council (ECOSOC)

Open PDF Go to committee

ECOSOC coordinates UN work on economic, social, and related policy issues. It provides a platform for dialogue and policy guidance across development, humanitarian, and sustainability agendas and connects UN entities with member states and civil society.

Delegates: Sofie Marielle Kowarsch, Lena Katherina Pitz.

For committee context, contacts, and delegate profiles, see the ECOSOC section on the delegation page.

Economic Commission for Latin America and the Caribbean (ECLAC)

Open PDF Go to committee

ECLAC is the UN’s regional commission for Latin America and the Caribbean. It supports evidence-based policy discussions on development challenges and regional cooperation, and it provides analytical work that informs decision-making and negotiation.

Delegates: Sophie Bade, Friederike van Garsel.

For committee context, contacts, and delegate profiles, see the ECLAC section on the delegation page.

United Nations Environment Assembly (UNEA)

Open PDF Go to committee

UNEA is the UN’s highest-level forum on environmental matters. It sets broad priorities for global environmental cooperation, considers policy responses to emerging challenges, and shapes the direction of work under the UN Environment Programme.

Delegates: Elena Xenia Mühl, Jonas Asmer.

For committee context, contacts, and delegate profiles, see the UNEA section on the delegation page.

Peacebuilding Commission (PBC)

Open PDF Go to committee

The Peacebuilding Commission supports international efforts to sustain peace in countries affected by conflict. It provides advice, helps improve coordination across actors, and promotes longer-term approaches that link political, security, and development considerations.

Delegates: Valentina Arnaldi, Jette Ewerhardy.

For committee context, contacts, and delegate profiles, see the PBC section on the delegation page.

UN Refugee Agency (UNHCR)

Open PDF Go to committee

UNHCR is the UN agency mandated to protect refugees and other displaced people. It works with states and partners on protection standards, practical assistance, and durable solutions, while bringing humanitarian and legal perspectives into policy debates.

Delegates: Hind Ben Yamoun, Samia Khan.

For committee context, contacts, and delegate profiles, see the UNHCR section on the delegation page.

Non-Proliferation Treaty Review Conference (NPT)

Open PDF Go to committee

The NPT Review Conference process assesses the implementation of the Treaty on the Non-Proliferation of Nuclear Weapons. It provides a forum to review commitments, debate priorities across non-proliferation, disarmament, and peaceful uses of nuclear energy, and to identify areas for collective progress.

Delegates: Alesia Babich, Yassin Malek.

For committee context, contacts, and delegate profiles, see the NPT section on the delegation page.
